
              <p/><p>Given a set of <i>non-overlapping</i> intervals, insert a new interval into the intervals (merge if necessary).</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>You may assume that the intervals were initially sorted according to their start times.</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>&#13;
<b>Example 1:</b><br/>&#13;
Given intervals <code>[1,3],[6,9]</code>, insert and merge <code>[2,5]</code> in as <code>[1,5],[6,9]</code>.&#13;
</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>&#13;
<b>Example 2:</b><br/>&#13;
Given <code>[1,2],[3,5],[6,7],[8,10],[12,16]</code>, insert and merge <code>[4,9]</code> in as <code>[1,2],[3,10],[12,16]</code>.&#13;
</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>&#13;
This is because the new interval <code>[4,9]</code> overlaps with <code>[3,5],[6,7],[8,10]</code>.&#13;
</p>
