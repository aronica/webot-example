
              <p/><p>The count-and-say sequence is the sequence of integers beginning as follows:<br/>&#13;
<code>1, 11, 21, 1211, 111221, ...</code>&#13;
</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>&#13;
<code>1</code> is read off as <code>"one 1"</code> or <code>11</code>.<br/>&#13;
<code>11</code> is read off as <code>"two 1s"</code> or <code>21</code>.<br/>&#13;
<code>21</code> is read off as <code>"one 2</code>, then <code>one 1"</code> or <code>1211</code>.<br/>&#13;
</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>&#13;
Given an integer <i>n</i>, generate the <i>n</i><sup>th</sup> sequence.&#13;
</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>&#13;
Note: The sequence of integers will be represented as a string.&#13;
</p>&#13;
