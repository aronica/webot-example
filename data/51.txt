
              <p/><p>The <i>n</i>-queens puzzle is the problem of placing <i>n</i> queens on an <i>n</i>Ã—<i>n</i> chessboard such that no two queens attack each other.</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p><img src="http://www.leetcode.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/8-queens.png"/></p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>Given an integer <i>n</i>, return all distinct solutions to the <i>n</i>-queens puzzle.</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>Each solution contains a distinct board configuration of the <i>n</i>-queens' placement, where <code>'Q'</code> and <code>'.'</code> both indicate a queen and an empty space respectively.</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>For example,<br/>&#13;
There exist two distinct solutions to the 4-queens puzzle:</p>&#13;
<pre>&#13;
[&#13;
 [".Q..",  // Solution 1&#13;
  "...Q",&#13;
  "Q...",&#13;
  "..Q."],&#13;
&#13;
 ["..Q.",  // Solution 2&#13;
  "Q...",&#13;
  "...Q",&#13;
  ".Q.."]&#13;
]&#13;
</pre>
