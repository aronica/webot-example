
              <p/><p>&#13;
Given a binary tree, flatten it to a linked list in-place.&#13;
</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>&#13;
For example,<br/>&#13;
Given&#13;
</p><pre>&#13;
         1&#13;
        / \&#13;
       2   5&#13;
      / \   \&#13;
     3   4   6&#13;
</pre>&#13;
&#13;
&#13;
The flattened tree should look like:<br/>&#13;
<pre>&#13;
   1&#13;
    \&#13;
     2&#13;
      \&#13;
       3&#13;
        \&#13;
         4&#13;
          \&#13;
           5&#13;
            \&#13;
             6&#13;
</pre>&#13;
&#13;

&#13;
<div class="spoilers"><b>Hints:</b>&#13;
<p>If you notice carefully in the flattened tree, each node's right child points to the next node of a pre-order traversal.</p>&#13;
