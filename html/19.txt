
              <p/><p>Given a linked list, remove the <i>n</i><sup>th</sup> node from the end of list and return its head.</p>&#13;
&#13;
<p>&#13;
For example,</p>&#13;
&#13;
<pre>&#13;
   Given linked list: <b>1-&gt;2-&gt;3-&gt;4-&gt;5</b>, and <b><i>n</i> = 2</b>.&#13;
&#13;
   After removing the second node from the end, the linked list becomes <b>1-&gt;2-&gt;3-&gt;5</b>.&#13;
</pre>&#13;
&#13;
<p>&#13;
<b>Note:</b><br/>&#13;
Given <i>n</i> will always be valid.<br/>&#13;
Try to do this in one pass.&#13;
</p>
